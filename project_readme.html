This web application provides real-world, population-based metrics for seasonal influenza using publicly available data from the CDC's Influenza Hospitalization Surveillance Network (FluSurv-NET). The platform is designed to bridge the gap in understanding seasonal flu dynamics by integrating multiple levels of analysis, including:

  • Spatial trends (geographic distribution of flu hospitalizations)
  • Social determinants of health (impact of socioeconomic and demographic factors, with simulation of the Social Vulnerability Index's effect on the epidemic curve)
  • Mathematical and statistical modeling (incidence rates, forecasting, and epidemic spread models)

By leveraging biostatistical, epidemiological, and machine learning techniques, this tool empowers public health researchers to assess flu patterns efficiently and make data-driven decisions for intervention strategies.

Key Features

1. Data Collection and Processing

  • Data Source: CDC FluSurv-NET
  • Coverage: Over 90 U.S. counties across 14 states, representing ~9% of the U.S. population (~30 million people)
  • Processing:
    • Raw data ingestion via R
    • Data cleaning, transformation, and aggregation
    • Statistical summarization (descriptive statistics, incidence rates, demographic stratifications)

2. Analytics & Modeling

  • Descriptive Statistics: Incidence, prevalence, demographic breakdowns
  • Predictive Modeling:
    • Time Series Forecasting (ARIMA, SARIMA)
    • Compartmental Models (SIR, SEIR) for disease spread simulations
    • Machine Learning: Logistic regression for outcome predictions
  • Geospatial Analysis: Flu spread visualization at a county/state level

3. End Users

Our tool is designed for researchers and organizations involved in population-based health outcomes research, including but not limited to:

  • Public health departments (e.g., University of Michigan, Emory University)
  • Government institutions (e.g., CDC, NIH)
  • Non-governmental organizations (NGOs) (e.g., Kaiser Permanente)
  • Biopharma and biotech companies (e.g., Merck, Pfizer, Eli Lilly)
